Raccoon

They often pick up the food item with their front paws to examine it and rub the item, sometimes to remove unwanted parts. This gives the appearance of the raccoon "washing" the food.

  • Type: Mammals
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Average life span in the wild: 2 to 3 years
  • Size: 60 to 95 cm
  • Weight: 1.8 to 10.4 kg
  • Group name: Nursery

The raccoon is a medium-sized mammal native to North America. Its grayish coat mostly consists of dense underfur which insulates it against cold weather. Two of the raccoon's most distinctive features are its extremely dexterous front paws and its facial mask, which are themes in the mythologies of the indigenous peoples of the Americas.
